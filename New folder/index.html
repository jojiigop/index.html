ABOUT DIGITAL ART

Description

Digital art refers to art created using digital technologies, such as computers, tablets, or smartphones. Artists use various software and tools to create images, illustrations, animations, or designs. It allows for a wide range of styles and techniques, from digital painting and photo manipulation to vector graphics and 3D modeling. Digital art offers flexibility, allowing artists to experiment easily, correct mistakes, and produce high-quality, reproducible works.

ORIGIN

Digital art originated in the 1950s and 1960s with early computer graphics experiments. Pioneering efforts were made by artists like Ben F. Laposky and later by institutions like MIT. The advent of personal computers in the 1980s and software like Adobe Photoshop in the 1990s greatly expanded its possibilities. Today, digital art is diverse and influential, thanks to technological advancements and the internet.
